What is Osteopathy?

Manual Therapy Approach

Osteopathy is a form of manual therapy that uses a range of hands-on techniques such as massage, mobilisation, manipulation as well as prescribing exercises to help treat the musculoskeletal system.

University Education

Osteopathy is a University degree encompassing Anatomy, Physiology, musculoskeletal diagnosis and Osteopathic treatment.

AHPRA Registered

Osteopaths in Australia are registered with the Allied Health Practitioner Regulation Agency (AHPRA).

Clinical Experience

Completing 1200 hours of supervised clinic practice before graduating (UK degree standard).

No Referral Needed

You do not need a GP referral to see an Osteopath for general treatment.

What to Expect on Your First Visit

Initial Assessment Process

On your first visit we will take a thorough medical history discussing your current complaint as well as any previous relevant medical history to start to establish a working diagnosis.

Various physical testing (both orthopaedic and neurological) will be done to establish your range of motion and determine what movements may be limited. Once we have a working diagnosis we will discuss with you the best treatment plan for you.

What to Wear

Please wear or bring loose clothing that you feel comfortable to move in or gym clothing such as shorts, t-shirt or leggings. Some disrobing may be required for examination.

Bring Reports

Please bring any imaging reports or X-rays that you may have related to your area of concern to your appointment.

Health Fund Card

Bring your health fund card as we can claim through HICAPS at the time of your appointment for eligible rebates.

Frequently Asked Questions

What should I expect during my first visit?

Your first appointment takes 60 minutes and includes a comprehensive health history, physical examination, diagnosis, and initial treatment. Please arrive 10 minutes early.

How many sessions will I need?

This varies based on your condition. Acute issues may resolve in 2-4 sessions, while chronic conditions may require a longer treatment plan. We'll discuss this during your initial assessment.

Do you accept health insurance?

Yes, we work with most major health insurance providers and can process claims through HICAPS. Please bring your health fund card to your appointment.

Do I need a referral from my GP?

No, you don't need a GP referral for general osteopathic treatment. However, referrals are required for some government schemes like DVA, Workcover, and Medicare AHMR.

Do you treat children?

Yes, we treat children aged 3-13 years with specialized consultation times and gentle techniques. We do not treat children under 3 years old.

What is your cancellation policy?

Cancellations with less than 24 hours notice incur a charge of 50% of the consultation fee. We appreciate as much notice as possible.
